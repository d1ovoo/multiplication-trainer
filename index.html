Mental Multiplication Techniques

📌 Method 1: Vedic Cross Multiplication (Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam)

This ancient Indian technique breaks down the multiplication into simple steps.

Example: 23 × 17

1
Rightmost digits: 3 × 7 = 21
Write 1, carry 2
2
Criss-cross: (2 × 7) + (3 × 1) = 14 + 3 = 17
Add carry: 17 + 2 = 19. Write 9, carry 1
3
Leftmost digits: 2 × 1 = 2
Add carry: 2 + 1 = 3
Result: 391

📌 Method 2: Breakdown Method

Split one number and multiply separately, then add results.

Example: 34 × 26

1
Break down: 26 = 20 + 6
2
Multiply each part:
34 × 20 = 680
34 × 6 = 204
3
Add together: 680 + 204 = 884
Result: 884

📌 Method 3: Number Close to Base

Useful when both numbers are near a round number (like 100).

Example: 98 × 97

1
Differences from 100:
98 is 2 below 100
97 is 3 below 100
2
Step 1: Subtract difference from other number
98 - 3 = 95 (or 97 - 2 = 95)
3
Step 2: Multiply the differences
2 × 3 = 6
4
Combine: 9500 - 6 = 9494
Result: 9506

💡 Tips for Faster Multiplication

  • Practice regularly: Start slow, speed comes with repetition
  • Memorize 2-digit squares: Numbers ending in 0-9 squared (10²-99²)
  • Break large numbers: Split into smaller, manageable chunks
  • Use reference numbers: Work from nearest round number (10, 20, 100, etc.)
  • Visualize: Picture the numbers in your mind as you calculate
  • Check answers: Use reverse estimation to verify results
